# Lesson 1 UX friction

## Evidence
Learner opened the course and noticed the Knowledge check area looked weird. That shows they are engaging with the page visually, not just accepting generated output.

## Challenges
The first pass used accessible native fieldset/legend semantics, but browser default rendering made the quiz look awkward. The issue was visual polish, not concept confusion.

## Engagement Pattern
Learner gives direct feedback on UI quality and expects corrections. They are likely to notice inconsistency or sloppy visual details.

## Adjust
For Lesson 2, inspect component rendering more carefully before presenting. Keep controls simple, visually polished, and beginner-friendly. Do not rely on semantic correctness alone; verify visual shape with static checks and screenshot-minded review.
